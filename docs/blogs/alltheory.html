Theory of Everything : All Physics, One Theory

Our curious human mind's fundamental nature is to question every essential aspect of the universe we live in and learn about them.
In the pursuit of such eternal knowledge, many intellectuals throughout our history have unraveled the mysteries of the universe.
People gained experience about the smallest subatomic particle electron to the largest black holes. Even after gaining so much knowledge, we still do not know anything
.

The recent baffling question among the physics community is, " What is the theory that can combine the properties of the four fundamental forces: Electromagnetic, Weak Nuclear, Strong Nuclear, and Gravity?"
or to put it simply: "The Theory of Everything." We all know that Gravity is a long-range force, and the other three are short-range forces.
Einstein's General Theory of Relativity justifies that Gravity is the curvature of the space-time fabric. Quantum Mechanics explain the origin of the other three fundamental forces through particle interactions and gauge invariance.
The 21st-century scientists try to establish a way to incorporate Einstien's field equations and Standard model particle equation to get a grand unified equation.
But Alas, they couldn't find it anyway! So it's time for a new theory that solves this grand unification problem.

The two competing theories to solve this are String Theory and Quantum Loop Gravity.
String theory states that every fundamental particle in our universe is made up of tiny strings vibrating in specific frequencies & patterns to generate the fundamental particles which create the universe.
String theory suggests that there are 11 dimensions, which is beyond the perception of 3D human beings.
In the ancient Hindu culture, it is said that god is an 11-dimensional total eternal energy; maybe it was not actually god, but string theory.
String theory also suggests that Gravity is caused by hypothetical particles called Gravitons, which provides a new particle perspective.
